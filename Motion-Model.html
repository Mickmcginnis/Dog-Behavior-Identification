Motion Model

Dynamics and setup

Data was collected using smart phone accelerometer readings recorded via the Matlab mobile app. For the duration of data collection, the phone was kept in place on the dog's back by a pocket stitched to the back of a sweater typically worn by the dog for warmth. The phone was positioned flat on the dog's back with the screen facing upward, its y-axis parallel to the dog's spine, its x-axis perpendicular to the dog's spine, and its z-axis vertical and perpendicular to the platform of the dog's back (see Figure 1).

As soon as Matlab began recording motion data, a video recording was initiated so sensor data could be correlated to the video's time stamp and the particular motion the dog was performing upon playback. As there are three separate measurements being taken - the acceleration in the x, y, and z axes - there are three degrees of freedom in data collection.

Behaviors and motion

We collected data for seven different dog behaviors, which are as follows:

  • Up

    For the "Up" motion, the dog jumps, lifting its front paws off the ground and propelling its posterior upward; if the dog is near another stable object or being, it uses its paws to stabilize itself against the object or being on its hind legs

  • Down

    The "down" movement entails the dog lowering itself to the ground by bending its legs from either a standing or sitting position

  • Sit

    The "sit" motion involves the dog beginning in a standing position and lowering its bottom down to the floor, coming to rest on its haunches.

  • Stand

    "Stand" is performed when the dog is stationary on four extended legs with its abdomen parallel to the ground

  • Walk

    For the "walk" movement the dog maintains a relaxed gait wherein at least two paws are on the ground at any given time; the dog propels itself forward often using its right front leg and back left leg (and vise-versa) synchronously

  • Trot

    The "trot" movement requires the dog to move forward, pushing off the ground with alternating feet as is done is "walk" but not maintaining contact between the ground and two of its paws

  • Run

    "Run" is performed when the dog propels itself using its back legs synchronously to launch itself forward and its front legs to extend forward to synchronously impact the ground
